Philosophy

Why Payments BA?

Cross-border payment investigations are among the most complex domains in banking — every SWIFT message, every recall flow, every approval threshold carries compliance and financial consequences. I started my career as a Business Analyst and have spent 5+ years building deep expertise across enterprise platforms and Tier-1 banking — from HCM and marketing tech to payment investigation platforms at JPMC, NatWest, and currently Goldman Sachs. I define how investigation platforms classify inbound MT and MX messages, route them to the right resolution activity, apply 4-eye verification controls, and send compliant outbound correspondence. I don't just document requirements — I shape the logic that determines whether a payment gets recovered, a non-receipt claim is resolved, or a recall succeeds.

Senior Business Analyst  ·  Pega Smart Investigate Agentic Automation (SIAA, Pega 24.2)  ·  Domain: Banking – Payments

Led requirements engineering for NatWest's enterprise cross-border payment-investigation platform on Pega SIAA (Pega 24.2) with Constellation UI — owning requirements across Dispatch, Orchestration, and Resolution Activity case types.

Intake & Routing

  • Defined intake across four channels: front-office (CSR), back-office (Payment Specialist), automated inbound SWIFT MT/MX messages, and NLP Email Bot that auto-creates Dispatch cases from payment emails.
  • Specified keyword and topic-extraction configuration for the email channel, including investigation-intent classification logic.

Resolution Activity Logic

  • Specified DetermineRAforIntent decision table covering all seven Resolution Activities: CCNR, RPNI, RFI/UTEX, CANC, RPTS, SAHM, and Generate Ad Hoc Adjustment.

SWIFT MT & ISO 20022 Correspondence

  • Authored functional requirements for outbound correspondence across MT103, MT192, MT199 and ISO 20022 / CBPR+ MX (pacs.008, pacs.004, camt.056, camt.110, camt.029).
  • Defined message-template field configuration (mandatory / editable / visibility) filtered by Line of Business and case intent.
  • Specified automated chaser schedules and duplicate-detection scoring model for STP vs. manual review.

Verification & Approval Controls

  • Defined 4-eye / 6-eye verification rules — thresholds triggering Supervisor vs. Manager approval before funds move or correspondence is sent.
  • Mapped Approve / Reject / Send-for-Repair workflow across CSR › Supervisor › Manager hierarchy.

SWIFT Case Management & Stop-and-Recall

  • Specified SRP requirements — camt.110, RQCL/RQST actions, camt.056 / camt.029 / MT199 recall flows, and cancellation reason-code set (DUPL, FRAD, CUST, AM09, etc.).

Reconciliation, Access & UAT

  • Documented nostro/vostro reconciliation requirements across five break types and three REST APIs integrating the reconciliation engine with SIAA.
  • Defined RBAC across eight access groups, work-queue and SLA setup per transaction type, and host-currency/FX configuration per branch.
  • Led UAT, defect triage, and business sign-off — zero critical post-release defects.
