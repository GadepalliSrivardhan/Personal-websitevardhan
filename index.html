NatWest — Cross-Border Payment Investigations & Exception Automation

Client: NatWest  |  Role: Senior Business Analyst  |  Platform: Pega Smart Investigate Agentic Automation (SIAA, Pega 24.2)  |  Domain: Banking – Payments

Led requirements engineering for NatWest's enterprise cross-border payment-investigation platform on Pega Smart Investigate Agentic Automation (SIAA, Pega 24.2) with Constellation UI — owning requirements across all three product case types: Initiate Payment Inquiry (Dispatch), Orchestration, and Resolution Activity.

Intake & Routing

  • Defined intake and routing requirements across four trigger channels: front-office (CSR Interaction Portal), back-office (Payment Specialist Web Portal), automated inbound SWIFT MT/MX messages, and an NLP Email Bot that auto-creates Dispatch cases from payment-related emails.
  • Specified keyword and topic-extraction configuration for the email channel, including investigation-intent classification logic.

Resolution Activity Logic

  • Specified the intent-to-resolution logic in the DetermineRAforIntent decision table covering all seven Resolution Activities: CCNR (Creditor Claims Non-Receipt), RPNI (Re-effect Payment), RFI/UTEX (Request for Information), CANC (Request Return of Funds), RPTS (Return Payment to Sender), SAHM (Send Ad Hoc Message), and Generate Ad Hoc Adjustment.

SWIFT MT & ISO 20022 Correspondence

  • Authored functional requirements for outbound correspondence across SWIFT MT (MT103, MT192, MT199) and ISO 20022 / CBPR+ MX (pacs.008, pacs.004, camt.056, camt.110, camt.029).
  • Defined message-template field configuration (mandatory / editable / visibility) filtered by Line of Business and case intent.
  • Specified automated chaser schedules (Chaser 1/2) for MT and email correspondence, and duplicate-detection scoring model governing straight-through linking vs. manual review.

Verification & Approval Controls

  • Defined 4-eye / 6-eye correspondence verification rules — which Resolution Activities and host-currency-equivalent amount thresholds trigger Supervisor vs. Manager approval.
  • Mapped the Approve / Reject / Send-for-Repair workflow across the CSR › Supervisor › Manager hierarchy.

SWIFT Case Management & Stop-and-Recall

  • Specified SWIFT Case Management and Stop-and-Recall (SRP) requirements — camt.110 investigation requests, replays, and RQCL/RQST actions, plus camt.056 / camt.029 / MT199 recall flows.
  • Defined the cancellation reason-code set (DUPL, FRAD, CUST, AM09, etc.) and routing of inbound SRP requests to manual Review-and-Action.

Reconciliation & Gap Analysis

  • Documented nostro/vostro reconciliation exception requirements across five break types and three REST APIs (Create / Update / Resolve Reconciliation Exception) integrating the reconciliation engine with SIAA.
  • Authored AS-IS/TO-BE process maps and formal gap analysis of product limitations (empty internal-payment paths, inactive fund-recovery stages) and raised them with the client ahead of go-live as development/configuration requirements.

Access, SLA & UAT

  • Defined RBAC across eight access groups and business personas, work-queue and SLA setup per transaction type (Internal vs. External Payment Inquiries), and host-currency/FX configuration per branch.
  • Led UAT, defect triage, and business sign-off using Insights dashboards validating Dispatch and Orchestration volumes by message and intent type.
  • Authored and maintained the Requirements Traceability Matrix across all releases — zero critical post-release defects.
